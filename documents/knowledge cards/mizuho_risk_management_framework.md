# Mizuho Risk Management Framework

## Metadata
- Institution: Mizuho Financial Group
- Source: Integrated Report 2025 (Risk Governance)
- Priority: High

## Purpose
Capture how Mizuho identifies, measures, monitors and manages enterprise risks and integrates them into business planning.

## Executive Summary
Mizuho's Risk Management Framework is built around the Risk Appetite Framework (RAF). Risk appetite is established before business planning, validated using stress testing, and monitored continuously. Material changes in the external environment may trigger reassessment of business plans and risk appetite.

## Core Principles
- Risk taking supports strategic objectives.
- Risk appetite guides business planning.
- Business plans are validated using stress testing.
- Continuous monitoring throughout the year.
- Management adjusts risk appetite when conditions materially change.

## Risk Appetite Framework (RAF)
Definition:
Risk appetite defines the types and levels of risk Mizuho is willing to accept while executing its strategy.

### RAF Cycle
1. Identify external environment and top risks
2. Determine risk appetite
3. Formulate business plans
4. Build baseline and adverse scenarios
5. Perform stress testing
6. Assess business plan resilience
7. Monitor risks continuously
8. Review and recalibrate
